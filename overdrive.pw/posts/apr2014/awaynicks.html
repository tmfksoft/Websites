Announcement regarding awaynick disabling

Posted by GLolol on 12 Apr 2014

After some debate with our operators and administrators, we have collectively decided to disable the use of awaynicks on our network.

The reason behind this decision is really for practicality. As IRC is an asynchronous protocol, there is really no need to iterate the fact that you're away by changing your nick or using an away message. Simply mark yourself away using the /away command, and anyone who tries to contact you will get a friendly message saying that you're busy.

* [synnerjee] is away (I'm busy)

So, what do I do?

Chances are (if you don't use awaynicks), this will not affect you at all.

For the ones who still do, this change will only block nick changes matching *[a]* and *[away]*, without impacting any other functionality.

If you still are using awaynicks, well, don't. Most clients (such as HexChat, WeeChat, and others), use the standard /away command, and don't announce away messages. ZNC users can simply unload module *awaynick (if loaded), and use *simple_away instead.

As always, the OVERdrive-IRC is open to your feedback regarding this issue. Feel free to drop us a comment in #Help if you have one.

Thanks for your continued support.
